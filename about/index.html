About.  

I am a software developer, recently graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science from UC San Diego. I am highly driven as a software engineer, and try to write highly readable, maintainable and efficient code. I want to design delightful experiences that touch the lives of many.

I am from Mumbai. After two years in Phnom Penh, I moved to San Diego for university in 2012. There, I worked with Learning Equality, an organization that is bringing the online learning revolution offline, and the minds behind KA Lite - an offline deployment of Khan Academy. I also did some web development for LegDay.

Meanwhile, I have worked on numerous personal projects; some small, some big. My portfolio includes an assortment of little scripts, libraries and tinkerings. It also includes Quibbler.co, an experimental group chat experience that allows anybody to write messages anonymously. These messages are visualized like a word cloud, and disappear forever in a few seconds.

In the summer of 2015, I worked as a software engineering intern at Amazon's Seattle headquarters. There, I gained my first experiences of working with enterprise scale web application development. I was in charge of a critical part of a new project cooked up by the Warehouse Deals & Liquidations team.

I have travelled around India and south-east Asia quite a bit. Other than programming and design, I am very enthusiastic about video games and movies.
