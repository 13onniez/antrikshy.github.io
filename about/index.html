About.  

I am a software developer, currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science at UC San Diego. I am highly driven as a software engineer, and try to write highly readable, maintainable and efficient code. I want to design delightful experiences that touch the lives of many.

I am originally from Mumbai. I moved to San Diego for university in 2012. There, I worked with Learning Equality, an organization that is bringing the online learning revolution offline, and the minds behind KA Lite - an offline deployment of Khan Academy. I have also did some web development for LegDay, a new startup that will connect people at the gym.

Meanwhile, I have worked on numerous personal projects; some small, some big. My portfolio includes an assortment of little scripts, libraries and tinkerings. It also includes Quibbler.co, an experimental group chat experience that allows anybody to anonymously write messages that are visualized like a word-cloud, only to permanently disappear after a few seconds.

In the summer of 2015, I worked as a software engineering intern at Amazon's Seattle headquarters. There, I picked up my first experiences working with enterprise scale web applications. I was in charge of a critical part of a massive liquidations related project cooked up by the Warehouse Deals & Liquidations team.
